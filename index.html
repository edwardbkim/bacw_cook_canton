Executive Summary (What the market is doing to Cook)

Peoria appears to be a labor-accessible and affordability-favorable market for Cook’s Canton location. Compared with Bloomington and Winston-Salem, unemployment is higher, assembler specialization is stronger, and the rent affordability threshold sits materially below assembler median wage. Together, those signals suggest a market with better near-term hiring flexibility and less housing-driven retention pressure than the other pilot MSAs.

Opportunity: stronger labor access than Bloomington and Winston-Salem Opportunity: assembler pay materially exceeds the rent threshold Risk: demand remains concentrated in QA, machining, and supervisory roles Risk: technical and lead roles likely require targeted recruiting
  • Primary advantage: Peoria combines labor availability with strong production specialization (assembler LQ 1.92).
  • Key constraint: hiring pressure appears concentrated in technical and supervisory roles rather than broad entry production demand.
  • Strategic implication: Cook can likely recruit entry production labor efficiently here, but should target technical pipelines with much more precision.

Strategic positioning: Bloomington is scarcity-driven, Winston-Salem is competition-driven, and Peoria appears access-driven with technical bottlenecks.

Wage Positioning (Peoria)

Peoria’s current role basket is more decision-useful than a historical wage-growth panel. The sharper question here is not how fast wages moved over time, but whether today’s wage structure supports hiring and retention across the labor stack. On that basis, Peoria appears favorable for entry production recruitment but still demanding for higher-skill roles.

  • Assembler median: $22.43/hr
  • Production median: $23.57/hr
  • QA/Inspection median: $26.20/hr
  • Machinist median: $24.24/hr
  • Maintenance median: $35.83/hr
  • Supervisor median: $32.81/hr

Strategic takeaway: Peoria looks wage-favorable for entry production hiring, but the premium curve for technical and leadership roles remains steep enough to justify role-specific recruiting strategies.

Housing Band Insights (Favorable Affordability Position)

Housing in the Canton/Peoria market appears materially less constraining than in Bloomington or Winston-Salem. The rent affordability threshold sits below assembler median wage, reducing one of the key retention frictions observed in the other pilots.

Affordability Method

Assumptions: 40 hrs/week, 52 weeks/year (= 2,080 hrs), using the 30% and 35% gross income rules. Hourly wage = (Monthly housing cost × 12) ÷ (Rule × 2,080).

Demand diagnosis

  • Hiring demand is concentrated in QA, supervisory, and machining roles.
  • Entry production roles show lower posting intensity relative to workforce size.
  • The market reflects targeted technical competition, not broad labor scarcity.
  • Cook’s likely constraint is role-specific hiring efficiency, not market-wide access.

Competitive Landscape (Med-Tech & Adjacent Employers)

The Canton/Peoria labor market appears less constrained by broad labor access and more shaped by competition for technically skilled production roles. For Cook, the implication is that employer differentiation should focus on skilled-role recruitment, advancement visibility, and stability rather than market-wide wage leadership.

  • Market structure: the production labor base is strong, but higher-value roles still appear contested.
  • Strategic implication: Cook may be able to recruit entry roles more efficiently here than in Bloomington or Winston-Salem.
  • Next layer: deepen benchmarking against med-tech and industrial competitors to identify where Cook can differentiate in technical hiring and long-term retention.

Recruitment & Retention Recommendations (Canton / Peoria MSA)

  • Exploit the favorable affordability position: entry production wages appear more sustainable relative to housing than in the prior pilot markets.
  • Target technical bottlenecks directly: prioritize QA, machinist, maintenance, and supervisory pipelines rather than relying on broad-based recruitment messaging.
  • Compete on skilled-role pathways: visible progression from entry production into QA, technical, or lead roles should strengthen retention.
  • Use differentiated sourcing: technical roles likely need distinct channels and more active outreach than assembler hiring.
  • Position Cook as a stable, long-term employer: stability and progression may outperform simple wage competition in this market.
